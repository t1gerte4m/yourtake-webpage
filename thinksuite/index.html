The Product

The Concept

Three layers working together to create a private intelligence network that delivers unique value and deepens connections.

1

Intelligence ▶

Rarefied insights from the network, personalized to each reader's world.

Deliver insights that don't exist anywhere else. Not curated news. Anyone can do that. Rarefied perspectives gathered directly from family offices, shared anonymously, communicated in a format that takes 3 minutes to scan.

Each reader's briefing is curated to their world. A real estate family only sees insights relevant to real estate. And each insight is framed so they immediately understand why it matters to them and what to do about it. No translation required. This is where AI creates genuine value.

2

Connection ▶

The intelligence product becomes a matchmaking engine.

When a reader finds an insight compelling, they can signal interest in the topic. ThinkSuite brokers connections (small group discussions, 1-on-1 introductions) around shared interests the content reveals.

Over time, even how someone engages with the content helps identify non-obvious connections. This extends your matchmaking ability far beyond personal conversations alone.

3

Participation ▶

Readers contribute back. The product improves with every edition.

Each edition includes a provocation (a challenging question or tension between perspectives) and a simple way to respond. Responses feed into the next edition.

Over time, this builds a longitudinal dataset of family office sentiment. Readers become contributors, contributors become more connected, and the product gets better with every edition.

Layer 1

Intelligence

Deliver insights that don't exist anywhere else, in a format that respects how busy these people are.

The Data Moat

There are two kinds of content you could create:

Low Value

Curating publicly available information through a family office lens. Anyone can do this. It's a filter on existing content.

High Value

Gathering rarefied insights directly from family offices and sharing them anonymously. This data doesn't exist anywhere else. No search can find it.

Smart Brevity Format

Each insight follows a layered structure. Scan in 3 minutes, go deep if you want.

The Insight

Family offices are quietly shifting 15-20% of portfolios into direct private credit deals, bypassing traditional fund structures.

Why This Matters to You

Personalized per reader. A real estate family sees: "This trend is creating competition for the same deal flow you're seeing in commercial real estate debt." A healthcare family gets different framing.

Go Deeper

Full anonymous source context, data behind the insight. Reader self-selects this depth.

Connect

"Want to discuss this with peers who are seeing the same thing? Let us know."

Not every insight goes to every reader. The briefing is filtered to what's relevant, then each insight is framed for that reader's world. Less noise, more signal. That's the core AI play.

Protecting Trust: The Privacy Model

Your community is deeply private. Many of your members would be uncomfortable with even basic recording or data collection. The entire intelligence model is designed around this reality:

Anonymous by default. No names, no identifying details, no attributable quotes, unless the contributor explicitly opts in.

Consent is conversational. Data is gathered through natural conversation at events, not surveillance. You ask permission in person: "Can I share this insight with the network anonymously?" No recording, no transcription. Just your notes.

Connection requests flow through you. A reader never gets direct access to a contributor. You broker every introduction, just like you do today. The contributor can always say no.

The trust you've built is the product. Nothing in this system works if it compromises that trust. Every design decision starts here.

Layer 2

Connection

The intelligence product doubles as a matchmaking engine, extending your personal touch at scale.

How It Works

Multiple mechanisms, ranging from zero reader effort to minimal effort:

Passive Matching (Zero Effort)

Track engagement patterns silently. Over time, cluster readers by interest. Surface match suggestions to you: "These 4 FOs all engage deeply with governance content. Consider introducing them." The reader did nothing. Their behavior revealed their interests.

Profile-Based Pre-Matching

You already know each family office. When new content is relevant to a specific FO's situation, reach out proactively: "I read something that made me think of you. Want me to connect you with the person behind it?" To the reader, it looks like your legendary personal touch. In reality, it's data-assisted.

One-Tap Reactions

Simple buttons on each section: "Spot on" / "Not what I'm seeing" / "Want to discuss." One tap. Over time, builds a detailed interest graph. Two readers who consistently react to the same content are strong match candidates.

The most powerful matchmaking happens when readers don't realize they're being matched. It feels like you always know exactly who should meet whom. The AI is invisible, amplifying your existing superpower.

Layer 3

Participation

Turn passive readers into active contributors without it feeling like work. Each response makes the next edition better.

The One-Question Close

Every edition ends with ONE provocative question. Not a survey. Just one question designed to be compelling enough that people want to answer. Reply to the email. That's it.

"What's the biggest mistake your family office made this year?"
"If you could only make one investment in 2026, what would it be?"
"What keeps you up at night about the next generation?"

The Provocation

Present a contrarian take or unresolved tension. People who disagree, or strongly agree, are naturally motivated to respond. Richer data than a question because they're responding to a position.

"One of our members believes family offices should be actively investing in AI companies right now. Another thinks it's a bubble. Where do you stand?"

Friction-Free Reactions

Three buttons at the bottom of each section. One tap. No text required. Aggregated reactions create a real-time sentiment layer for the next edition: "73% of our network agrees with this take."

Spot on Not what I'm seeing Want to discuss

Over 6-12 months, this builds a longitudinal dataset of family office sentiment. That dataset becomes the foundation for dossiers, matchmaking, and potentially a premium benchmark product.

Why Interviews First, Surveys Later

The instinct is to send a survey right away; it's fast and scales easily. But for this audience, that's the wrong starting point.

Start: In-Person Interviews

You already have the venue. Your events are the data collection system. Through natural conversation, you learn what people actually think, not what they'd check on a form.

Interviews reveal what questions you should eventually ask at scale. They surface topics you wouldn't have thought to include in a survey. And they deepen the relationship. People feel heard, not processed.

Later: Targeted Surveys

Surveys work once the newsletter proves value. When members have seen how their peers' insights show up in the product, they'll understand what they're contributing to.

Position it as: "Help shape the next edition. Your peers are reading this." Now the survey isn't a cold ask. It's participation in something they've already seen work. Response rates will be dramatically higher.

Physical Product

The Dossier

A quarterly, premium printed deep dive. It arrives in the mail. It sits on a desk. It signals membership in something exclusive.

Why Physical?

Premium signaling. A printed briefing on heavy stock paper says "you are part of something exclusive" in a way email never can.

Privacy. Physical is harder to leak than digital. You can't screenshot and forward it to 50 people.

Attention. Something in the mail commands a different quality of attention than one of 200 emails.

Specs

Heavy stock paper, minimalist typography. Think Berkshire Hathaway annual report, not glossy magazine.

Numbered editions: "Edition 1 of 85." Reinforces scarcity.

No ads, no sponsors, no external logos. Custom ThinkSuite mark on envelope.

QR code links to digital companion for reactions and connection requests.

Revenue & Growth

Business Case

How this product strengthens your existing business and creates new revenue opportunities.

Reinforces Your Core Business

Justifies premium membership pricing. Today, members pay for access to events and your network. Adding a tangible, recurring intelligence product gives them something they can point to: "This alone is worth the membership." It makes the value concrete and harder to walk away from.

Drives event attendance. The newsletter previews topics and surfaces questions that make people want to show up in person. Readers who engage with content about succession planning are primed to attend the next event where that topic comes up.

Expands your reach beyond geography. Events are local. The intelligence product reaches family offices globally. A family office in Singapore who can't attend a New York breakfast can still be part of the network, and they might fly in for the right event.

Deepens retention. Members who receive personalized intelligence, contribute insights, and get introduced to peers through the product are deeply embedded. The switching cost isn't financial; it's relational. They'd lose access to a system that knows them.

Geographic Expansion
