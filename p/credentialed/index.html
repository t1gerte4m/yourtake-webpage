The Current Reality at 45+ Executives

Fragmented Monitoring

Quoted, Harrow, Google Alerts, LinkedIn, industry newsletters. Each a separate tab, each requiring manual review. Multiply by 45+ executives and the team is scanning instead of strategizing.

Linear Scaling

Every new executive profile adds the same labor. The team that manages 10 needs proportionally more people to manage 50. Margins compress as the roster grows.

Reactive, Not Proactive

By the time your team spots a media opportunity in a HARO digest, the deadline may have passed. Manual processes operate on a delay. The authority game rewards speed.

You said it directly: "I don't want my team spending weeks manually updating anymore." Here's what replaces that.

Six Intelligence Layers, One Platform

Each layer solves a specific part of the Credentialed workflow. Together, they give your team a single place to monitor, plan, execute, and measure across every executive in the portfolio.

1

Real-Time Conversation Monitoring

Replaces: manual Quoted, Harrow, and Google Alerts scanning

Track what's being said across each executive's industry, topics, and competitor landscape. Surface opportunities the moment they appear: a reporter requests sources on a topic your executive owns, an industry conversation breaks where your client has authority, a competitor gets quoted and your executive should respond.

Being first to respond to a reporter request or first to comment on a trending conversation is the difference between getting quoted and getting ignored. The platform watches continuously so your team doesn't have to.

How it works + data sources

How it works

  • Per-executive topic maps define what's relevant (configurable, evolves with the program)
  • Relevance scoring filters thousands of daily signals down to the 5-10 that matter per executive
  • Alerts delivered via dashboard digest or real-time notification, configurable per client
  • Historical tracking: which opportunities were surfaced, pursued, and landed

Potential data sources

Media monitoring APIs Journalist request platforms Social listening feeds Podcast transcript analysis News aggregation Industry newsletter parsing
2

Reporter & Beat Intelligence

Replaces: manual reporter research and Quoted browsing

Know every reporter covering each executive's domain. See what they've published recently, who they tend to quote, which outlets they write for, and when they're likely to need sources.

When a reporter at Forbes who covers fintech leadership publishes a new piece, and your client is a fintech CEO, that connection should surface automatically. Not wait for someone on your team to spot it.

How it works + data sources

How it works

  • Reporter profiles built from byline analysis across thousands of publications
  • Beat mapping: what each reporter covers, their 90-day coverage arc, preferred source types
  • Outreach tracking: who was pitched, when, response status, outcome (quoted / not quoted / no response)
  • Match scoring: executive expertise overlap + outreach history = pitch priority ranking
  • Relationship intelligence: flag when a reporter quotes a competitor but hasn't been pitched your executive

Potential data sources

Muck Rack Qwoted Publication RSS feeds Byline tracking HARO / Featured.com Custom web research

Layers 1 and 2 together replace the most time-intensive part of the current workflow. The hours your team spends scanning and matching become automated intelligence, freeing them for the relationship work that actually lands placements. Walk me through your current process →

3

LinkedIn Intelligence

Replaces: manual monitoring of 45+ executive LinkedIn profiles

See the full LinkedIn picture for every executive in the portfolio. What they're posting about, what's performing, where the gaps are, how they compare to peers in their space.

At 45+ executives, you can't manually track engagement rates, identify content themes, or benchmark against competitors. The intelligence layer does this continuously and surfaces what matters: this executive's audience responds to leadership stories but they haven't posted one in 6 weeks. This executive's engagement dropped after shifting topics. This peer in their industry is gaining authority on a topic your client should own.

This layer exists today. YourTake already manages voice profiles per executive, generates research-backed content across multiple formats, runs campaign planning, and tracks performance with weekly AI-powered checkpoints. This isn't a roadmap item. It's live.
What's already built

Live capabilities

  • Per-executive voice profiles: paste 3-5 LinkedIn posts, AI extracts their voice automatically. Refine in plain English.
  • Content generation: on-brand drafts in each executive's voice across text, carousel, video script, and infographic formats. Credible sources woven in.
  • Five creation workflows: start from an idea, trending topics, content gap analysis, meeting notes/transcripts, or repurpose existing content
  • Campaign planning: set goals, define audience, AI researches themes, builds a week-by-week content calendar
  • Performance tracking: log engagement metrics per post, weekly AI reviews surface what's working and recommend adjustments
  • Peer benchmarking and topic gap detection across the portfolio

What this means for Credentialed

  • Each executive profile takes under an hour to set up (LinkedIn posts + website + positioning inputs)
  • Consistent voice across 45+ executives, even when multiple team members contribute content
  • Content delivery becomes repeatable instead of artisanal

4

Cross-Portfolio Topic Intelligence

Replaces: manual topic coordination across executives

When you manage 45+ executive authority programs, the question isn't just "what should this executive talk about?" It's "what should this executive talk about that nobody else in the portfolio owns yet?"

Map what every executive covers. Find the whitespace: topics where the industry is moving but none of your clients have authority. Spot overlap before two of your own executives compete for the same space. And when a topic works for one executive, cross-pollinate it to others in adjacent domains.

How it works + data sources

How it works

  • Topic clustering across all executive content portfolios to map who owns what
  • Industry trend detection via news, social media, and publication monitoring
  • Whitespace mapping: topics no one on the roster owns yet
  • Overlap detection: flag when multiple executives compete for the same topic space
  • Cross-pollination: when a topic performs for one executive, recommend it to others in adjacent domains
  • Trend velocity scoring: identify emerging topics before they peak, so your executives lead the conversation

Potential data sources

Executive content archives Industry publication feeds Social trend signals Competitive intelligence Topic clustering models

5

AI Search Visibility (GEO)

New capability: no one is doing this for individual executives yet

When someone asks ChatGPT "who are the leading voices in fintech regulation?" or asks Perplexity about B2B marketing strategy, does your client show up?

Generative Engine Optimization is the next frontier of executive visibility. Traditional search volume is projected to drop 25% by 2026 as AI-generated answers replace link-based results. A handful of firms are starting to offer GEO for brands. Almost no one is doing it for individual executives. That's a window, and Credentialed is perfectly positioned to own it.

"We don't just get you quoted in Forbes. We get you cited by ChatGPT."

How it works + data sources

How it works

  • Systematic querying across AI platforms: ChatGPT, Perplexity, Google AI Overview, Gemini
  • Per-executive citation tracking: when and where AI references them, in response to which types of queries
  • Entity strength scoring: how well each AI platform "knows" this executive
  • Content gap analysis: what's missing from the web that would make AI more likely to cite them
  • Optimization recommendations: content formats, distribution strategies, and structured data to improve visibility

Potential data sources

AI platform querying Web presence audits Entity recognition Knowledge graph analysis GEO tools (AthenaHQ, Semrush AIO)

Why this matters now

  • GEO tools are emerging rapidly. The platform integrates the best available tools rather than building from scratch
  • First-mover advantage: executives who build AI visibility now compound authority while competitors are still figuring out the space
  • This becomes a new selling point for Credentialed: "We don't just get you quoted in Forbes. We get you cited by ChatGPT."
6

Career Trajectory Roadmapping

Replaces: manual tracking in spreadsheets

The Credentialed program is ultimately about career advancement. An SVP becomes a General Counsel. A startup founder becomes a recognized industry voice. A technical leader becomes a conference keynote regular.

Each executive has a destination. The platform maps the path from where they are to where they want to be, tracking every milestone: speaking engagements landed, media placements secured, award nominations, LinkedIn authority growth, AI citation improvements. Progress becomes visible, ROI becomes provable, and the next recommended action is always clear.

How it works + data sources

How it works

  • Goal-setting framework: define career targets, authority milestones, and timeline per executive
  • Milestone tracking: speaking (applied, accepted, completed), media (pitched, published), awards (submitted, won), LinkedIn metrics, AI citations
  • Authority score: composite metric across all tracked dimensions, benchmarked against goals
  • Trajectory visualization: current state to target state with progress indicators per dimension
  • Automated recommendations: "Based on your authority in Topic X, target Speaking Event Y next quarter"
  • ROI reporting: investment vs. outcomes, making results like the SVP-to-General-Counsel story systematic and repeatable

Potential data sources

Manual input + CRM pipeline Media placement tracking Speaking event databases Award nomination tracking LinkedIn analytics AI citation data
